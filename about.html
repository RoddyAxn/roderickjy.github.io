About Roderick John Young

Roderick John Young is a Hamilton-based candidate, researcher, broadcaster, and public policy thinker focused on energy systems, infrastructure, governance, constitutional development, and long-term regional planning in New Zealand. He holds a law degree, LLB, science degree, B.Sc., social science degree, B.Soc.Sc (Hons), and a post graduate deploma in public policy, P.G. DIP Public Policy.

His work combines interests in economics, engineering systems, law, democratic accountability, and future-focused civic development. He has participated in public discussions surrounding Hamilton infrastructure, electricity affordability, hydrogen development, transport resilience, and community governance structures.

Roderick has been involved in public commentary and local governance discussions relating to the Waikato region, including energy policy, infrastructure financing, local government accountability, and strategic regional development. He regularly contributes to community conversations through public meetings, independent research, and media engagement.

He attends and contributes to Free FM community radio broadcasts in Hamilton, discussing issues including energy security, infrastructure planning, governance reform, technological transition, and regional economic resilience.

A major intellectual focus of his work is Solar System Jurisprudence, a developing theoretical framework connecting law, energy, civilisation, economics, thermodynamics, democratic stewardship, and long-term human development.

Solar System Jurisprudence explores the relationship between:

  • Energy and civilisation
  • Infrastructure and prosperity
  • Law and resource stewardship
  • Technology and democratic governance
  • Economic systems and long-term sustainability
  • Human institutions and planetary development

The framework proposes that legal and political systems ultimately operate within deeper physical and energetic constraints shaped by nature, infrastructure, science, and civilisation itself.

Roderick’s broader interests include:

  • Hydrogen infrastructure development
  • Electricity affordability
  • Regional resilience planning
  • Transport and logistics systems
  • Future energy technologies
  • Constitutional development in New Zealand
  • Hamilton and Waikato infrastructure strategy
  • Public accountability and transparent governance

His website serves as a developing public archive of ideas, policy concepts, commentary, infrastructure analysis, radio participation, and future-oriented civic discussion.
